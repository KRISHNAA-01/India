India: A Glorious Journey Through Time

How India Became India

India gained independence from the British Empire on 15th August 1947 and became a Republic on 26th January 1950. The country adopted its Constitution and became the world's largest democracy.

National Symbols


Flag of India

The Indian national flag, also known as the "Tiranga" (meaning tricolor), is a horizontal tricolor with India saffron (Kesari) at the top, white in the middle, and India green at the bottom. In the center of the white band is a navy-blue wheel with 24 spokes, called the Ashoka Chakra. The ratio of the flag's width to its length is 2:3.

State Emblem

The State Emblem of India is an adaptation of the Lion Capital of Ashoka at Sarnath, a sculpture dating back to the 3rd century BC. It features four lions standing back-to-back on a circular abacus, with a Dharma Chakra (wheel) in the center, and a bull and a horse on either side. The emblem also includes the motto "Satyameva Jayate" (Truth alone triumphs) inscribed below in Devanagari script.

National Anthem

"Jana Gana Mana" is the national anthem of India, written by Rabindranath Tagore.

National Song

"Vande Mataram" composed by Bankim Chandra Chatterjee, is the national song of India.
